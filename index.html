Minor Issues

Here is a list of all of our minor issues we have experienced, we separated these by issues that are more of a nuisance than a major problem that could be extremely costly or unsafe.

Garage Pools Water

Our garage has always pooled water in it after rain or snow melt. Not a huge deal, but certainly an annoyance to squeege out frequently after bad weather. This is within spec according to communications with M/I warranty department.

Leaky irrigation faucet & hose bib

In addition to all of the all leaks we have had, our irrigation system was leaky from the moment we turned it on, and a separate hose bib started leaking around a year after moving in. Both were fixed.

Owner Bathtub Panel Issues

The above photo was taken during construction, however you can see in the lower left of the photo that the access panel is popped out. After we closed on the house it was still popped out like this, and refused to "click" into place. After two visits the plumbing company came out and replaced the panel.

Sagging Internal Window Trim

Our window-woes don't end with the major ones listed in the section above. We've had minor issues with other windows like sagging trim on the interior of the window. The window company came out and removed the window, stuffed the trim back in it (hopefully secured it better, I'm not sure) and the issue has been resolved since that time. At the time of this writing another window has an identical issue to the one that was fixed previously. After contacting M/I warranty about this they let me know that my warranty has expired, however, there is still a manufacturers warranty and I need to work with the manufacturer. It's very scary that we are just out of warranty and having things go wrong with a 1.5 year old house.

Exposed to the Elements

During construction our home sat for months being exposed to the elements, no boards were put up over windows, the Tyvek wrap was tearing all around the home, and snow and rain were consistently getting into the home during construction.

Window Trim Uneven

After closing we noticed that the trim above our windows was uneven. The gap on the left side of the image above is much smaller than on the right side.

Messes

During construction of our home we often noticed small messes, like this image of our sink having been used without the courtesy of at least rinsing it.

Cracked and Dinged Exterior Cement Boards

After closing on our home we noticed several areas where our cement boards were cracked in half, had dings, or had other damage to them.

Faucets and Showerheads Popped from Wall

All of our faucets and showerheads on the upper level have had this issue where they popped out of the wall and cannot easily be pressed back in. One bathroom has been fixed while we were out of the one year warranty to have the other fixed by the time it occurred there.

Warped Trim

The trim board above our garage was severely warped where we could squeeze a finger through in some areas. After many four or five appointments from different individuals this issue was finally resolved.

Poor Trim Alignment

Throughout the home the trim is aligned poor and is missing paint where two corner pieces meet.

Nicks and Dings in the Trim

Throughout the home the trim had dings and nicks. We tried to catch as much of this as we could up front before closing. The "fix" for this was to simply apply some putty over these which is still visible.

Paint Drips and Marks

During our final walk through of the home we noticed many areas like this which had paint drips or marks in areas they were not supposed to be. Many of these were addressed, however, I put this one on us for not spending even more time to find more of these issues. After marking off hundreds of spots on the cabinets, walls, floors, and ceilings there were inevitably spots we missed. We should have demanded another walk through or more time, but we did not.

Missing Sod

Around one to two months after moving in we finally got sod. Except they did not fully sod our yard up to the property markers and were off by several feet as you can see by where our fence is compared to where the sod is.

In Conclusion

Our experience has been less than thrilling. One of the things to consider is all of the time you need to set aside to have these issues addressed from initial reporting, to scheduling, to having contractors come out and fix it (often taking many appointments fix a single issue) and making this work around your work schedule.

Many of our issues were addressed by M/I Homes and have since been resolved. This website is in no way saying that other homes they have or are building are going to have any of these issues, rather it is a summary of the experience and issues that we have had at our home.
