Lights Out | 2022 to 2023

A month or two after we moved in we quickly noticed a big issue... the power in our basement went out regularly. The entire basement is on one GFCI line. This GFCI outlet consistently popped which shut off all power to the entire line - meaning our internet would go out. Our water heater would shut off. Our freezer full of food would shut off. Our air exchanger would shut off. Literally everything in our basement that uses power was gone when this occurred.

At least it's easy to push the button in and reset the outlet, right?

Wrong. The outlet would refuse to reset. This would last anywhere from the shortest time of 5 hours to the longest time of it being out an entire weekend without resetting. This issue always presented itself in rainy or snowy weather. We had electrician after electrician come out every time this happened. They had theories but could not figure out what the issue was other than "hey it looks like it's working now" (as it often takes days to get someone out). We were told it must be our freezer drawing too much. To prove that it was not our freezer we decided to run an extension cable through our house down to the freezer.

Pop! It happened again, our freezer was off the line. After dealing with this issue for a year and half and proving it was not our freezer I demanded they send someone out immediately while the issue was happening (which means an emergency call for them versus a scheduled call - aka it costs more money).

Voila! The electrician came out and found that - and you won't believe this if you've read about our other major issues - water was leaking into the air exchanger and causing it to short out. The plumbing company was called to come repair the unit. They came out and attempted to repair it (and prevent water from getting into it). Their intial repair did not work as the unit kept shorting out. Then they came back and replaced the air exchanger with a new one. The issue has not occurred in roughly 6 months since the unit replaced.
